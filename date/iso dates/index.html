ISO Date Format in Detail

YYYY-MM-DDTHH:mm:ss.mssz

  1. YYYY: represents the year with four digits(e.g., 2022)
  2. MM: represents the month with two digits(e.g., 01 for January)
  3. DD: represents the day with two digits(e.g., 01 for the first day of the month)
  4. T: represents the starting of time after date format
  5. HH: represents hours with two digits (24-hour format)
  6. mm: represents minutes with two digits
  7. ss: represents seconds with two digits
  8. sss: represents the milliseconds with three digits
  9. Z: represents the time zone offset inn the form of Â±HH:mm(e.g., +05:30 for UTC+5:30) also we can write only Z, it means UTC time

Here are some examples,

  1. 2024-03-06T08:23:06.682Z(Z indicates that the time is expressed in UTC)
  2. 2024-03-06T18:23:06.682+05:30("+05:30" indicates that this time is 5 hours and 30 minutes ahead of UTC)
  3. 2024-03-06T18:23:06.682-07:00("-07:00" indicates that this time is 7 hours behind UTC")
